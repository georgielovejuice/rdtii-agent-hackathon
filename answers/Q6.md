The LLM is allowed to classify a provision against one specific RDTII indicator, extract a verbatim quote from the supplied legal text, flag ambiguity, and return UNCERTAIN when the evidence is insufficient. It operates as a constrained classifier, not as a general legal analyst.

The LLM is not allowed to score without a citation, infer regulatory intent beyond what is written, synthesize across multiple sources, or use Tier 3 material for scoring. Guidelines and FAQs may help locate relevant provisions, but they cannot support a confirmed score.

Every claim links to exact evidence through a fixed chain: verbatim quote, span verifier, citation record in SQLite. The verifier checks entity grounding and relation preservation against the source text. The citation record stores the URL, article reference, date when available, SHA256 hash, authority tier, score, confidence, and rationale.

A concrete failure case is the Paraphrase Trap. A Tier 3 ministry guideline says "data must be kept within the country," while a Tier 1 law says "transfers abroad require authorization." Our system assigns tiers to both sources, scores only from Tier 1, logs Tier 3 as located but not used for scoring in the audit trail, and outputs only the Tier 1 citation with a verbatim quote.
