Our evidence method is built around the rule "No Quote, No Score." Every confirmed score must include an exact verbatim quote from the legal text, the source name, authority tier, article or section reference, source URL, document SHA256 hash, and effective or document date when available. If any required evidence field is missing, the score is rejected as confirmed output and routed to the human review queue.

The LLM is not trusted as a source of legal truth. It is used only as a classifier against one RDTII indicator and one article-level chunk at a time. After it proposes a score and quote, the span verifier checks whether the quote appears in the source document after normalization for whitespace and minor punctuation differences. It also checks entity grounding for article numbers, law names, dates, and obligations.

If the verifier cannot find the quote or detects an unsupported entity, the score becomes UNCERTAIN. UNCERTAIN items remain visible in the output, but they are not auto-published as confirmed scores. This makes uncertainty explicit and gives a human reviewer the exact source and indicator needing review.
