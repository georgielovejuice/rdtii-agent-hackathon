Our system uses a six-stage pipeline. First, it collects sources from seeded official government sites and future crawler targets using Scrapy and Playwright, then ranks each source by authority tier. Official legislation and gazettes are Tier 1, amendments and implementing regulations are Tier 2, and guidelines or FAQs are Tier 3.

Second, it extracts legal text. Digital PDFs are handled with Docling, scanned PDFs with Surya OCR and Tesseract fallback, and HTML pages with BeautifulSoup. The extractor preserves article or section boundaries so scoring happens at provision level.

Third, the authority resolver filters the evidence base. Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources are scoreable; Tier 3 sources can only help locate relevant provisions. Conflicts are logged and routed to human review.

Fourth, retrieval matches article-level chunks to RDTII Pillar 6 and 7 indicators using ChromaDB semantic search when available, keyword fallback when offline, and a NetworkX knowledge graph for cross-references.

Fifth, a constrained Ollama LLM, `llama3.1:8b`, scores against hardcoded RDTII rubric JSON. The model may classify and quote, but not freestyle.

Finally, every quote is span-verified and exported as JSON-LD, a Jinja2 country brief, a SQLite audit trail, and a human review queue for UNCERTAIN items.
