Our demo covers Thailand, Vietnam, and Singapore across Pillars 6 and 7. Thailand uses the Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 from the Office of the Attorney General and related ETDA sources, with Thai and English materials. Vietnam uses the Cybersecurity Law, especially Article 26, and Decree 13/2023/ND-CP on Personal Data Protection, with Vietnamese and English text. Singapore uses the Personal Data Protection Act 2012, amended in 2020, from the Attorney-General's Chambers and PDPC sources, in English.

The system is designed for digital PDFs, scanned PDFs, and HTML pages. Digital PDFs are extracted with Docling, scanned documents with Surya OCR and Tesseract fallback, and HTML pages with BeautifulSoup or Playwright-backed discovery.

We apply a three-tier authority hierarchy. Tier 1 covers official legislation and gazettes. Tier 2 covers amendments and implementing regulations with legal force. Tier 3 covers guidelines, FAQs, and explanatory notes; these are useful for locating provisions but never used for scoring.

The three countries were chosen because they show a meaningful scoring range. Singapore is comparatively open but has conditional transfer rules. Thailand has conditional cross-border transfer and domestic compliance obligations. Vietnam includes stronger localization and physical presence obligations, making it a restrictive benchmark.
